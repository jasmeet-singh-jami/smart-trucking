# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Trucking company owners and operators across Canada and the US-Canada corridor — primarily small to mid-sized carriers managing compliance overhead themselves. Many are first-generation business owners for whom regulatory complexity is a real pain point. They use the site to find the right service, understand what's involved, and get in contact quickly. They are practical, time-pressured, and trust comes from professionalism and clarity, not visual flash.

## Product Purpose

smartrucking.ca is a lead-generation and self-service information site for Smart Trucking Services Inc., a Brampton, ON consulting firm specializing in trucking compliance, permits, authorities, and training since 2014. Success means phone calls and contact form submissions from qualified carriers. Secondary goals: let visitors self-serve the right service page, download forms, and access the employee/client portals without distraction.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, efficient, trustworthy. The brand is a knowledgeable neighbor — not a bureaucracy and not a slick SaaS product. It earns confidence through competence and clarity, not through marketing theatrics.

## Anti-references

Aggressive trucking industry sites: dark moody backgrounds, chrome trim, tire tread textures, photoshopped big-rig art, excessive macho energy. The redesign is explicitly a departure from that category. Also avoid: startup SaaS particle animations, gradient text, glassmorphism as decoration, and anything that reads as template-corporate.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity converts.** A carrier reading the site during dispatch should understand what we do in 10 seconds and know how to reach us. Nothing earns priority over that.
2. **Professionalism without stiffness.** The design should feel organized and capable — not cold or bureaucratic. Approachable expertise.
3. **Light earns trust.** The light minimalist direction (white backgrounds, clean type, restrained orange accent) deliberately rejects the aggressive trucking-industry aesthetic. Don't drift back toward it.
4. **Every section earns its place.** No filler, no duplicated headlines, no generic copy. Each section answers a specific visitor question.
5. **Consistency is credibility.** A site that's inconsistent in spacing, color, or copy reads as amateur. Trucking clients are skeptical — visual coherence signals that you sweat the details on their paperwork too.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigation must work throughout. Focus indicators must be visible. Touch targets 44x44px minimum on mobile.
